Welcome to the Hub!

This isn't just an English course. It's a place where we explore big ideas, ask tough questions, and connect what we learn to the world around us. Let's see how it all fits together.

Our First Two Adventures

Unit 1: How Music Shapes Our Lives

We'll explore how music influences our emotions, analyze trends in the music industry, and learn how sound affects our focus and well-being.

Unit 2: What is the Value of Money?

We'll investigate why money exists, debate the ethics of wealth and charity, and understand the economic forces that change prices.

The Collaboration Hub

This Course

The central hub for connecting ideas and developing critical skills.

Theory of Knowledge

To TOK: We'll ask: How do we know what a song *really* means? How does shared knowledge give money its value?

From TOK: You'll use our articles as real-world examples to analyze knowledge claims and explore the reliability of different sources.

Literature: Fahrenheit 451

To Literature: We'll discuss how consumerism and mass media in *Fahrenheit 451* prevent critical thought.

From Literature: You'll use our language for making recommendations (Unit 1) and discussing ethics (Unit 2) to analyze character decisions.

Sustainability

To Sustainability: We'll explore the environmental impact of music streaming (Unit 1) and the concept of "fast fashion" (Unit 2).

From Sustainability: You'll use our language for describing trends to analyze data on carbon emissions and our persuasive language skills to create environmental campaigns.

The Skills You'll Build

This course is designed to sharpen your abilities in critical thinking, clear communication, creative problem-solving, and effective collaboration.
